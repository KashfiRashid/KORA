# KORA — 90-Day Launch & PR Plan
*Bootstrapped, three founders, Vancouver-anchored. Built from the competitor research: Gymshark's community-first playbook + Lululemon/Represent's premium restraint, applied to a brand with no ad budget.*

Core principle (from the research): **community is the brand from day one.** You don't have ad money — you have a Vancouver gym network and a genuine story. That's the whole strategy.

---

## The spine: one hero product, launched as an event

Don't drop a catalog. Get **The Everyday Training Tee** exactly right and make its launch a moment. Everything below builds tension toward that single drop.

---

## Phase 1 — Build in public (Days 1–30)

**Goal:** turn the founding story into content and start a waitlist. No product for sale yet.

- **Set the channels.** Instagram + TikTok under the same handle. Bio: "The world's best cotton, kept. Bangladesh → Vancouver. Drop 01 loading." Link to the site waitlist.
- **Founder story content (your lane).** Short, honest videos: why three Bangladeshi friends are doing this, the "best fabric in the world leaves home as fast fashion — so we kept it" hook. Film the fabric, the sourcing, the sketches. Raw > polished.
- **Content pillars (rotate):**
  1. *Origin* — Bangladesh cotton, the craft, the why.
  2. *The make* — fabric tests, gsm, fit samples, behind-the-scenes.
  3. *The life* — training in Vancouver, the people it's for.
  4. *The point of view* — quiet design, quality over quantity, anti-fast-fashion.
- **Waitlist engine.** Every post drives to the site's "Notify me." Goal: 300–500 emails before launch.
- **Seed the inner circle.** Privately line up 15–30 **founding athletes** — real Vancouver lifters with taste and small-but-engaged followings (not mega-influencers). Treat them as teammates (Gymshark model): involve them now, before there's anything to sell.

## Phase 2 — Tease the drop (Days 31–60)

**Goal:** convert audience into anticipation.

- **Reveal the Tee.** Use the Higgsfield/real product shots. Show the fabric obsession — gsm, long-staple cotton, garment dye, the trained-fit.
- **Founding-athlete teasers.** Send samples to the 15–30. They post honest first impressions in their own voice. Repost everything.
- **Email sequence** to the waitlist: (1) the story, (2) the product, (3) founding-member offer + exact drop date/time.
- **Set scarcity honestly.** Limited first run ("Drop 01, made properly, limited"). Real limitation, not fake urgency.
- **PR groundwork (your lane):** build a simple press kit (one-pager + the OG cover + product shots + founder story). Target: Vancouver lifestyle/startup press, BD-diaspora media, fitness newsletters, and niche menswear/fitfashion accounts. Angle that sells: *"Bangladeshi founders flip the fast-fashion script — keeping the world's best cotton instead of exporting it cheap."* That's a story, not an ad.

## Phase 3 — Launch + sustain (Days 61–90)

**Goal:** sell the first run, prove the model, set up repeat.

- **Launch as an event.** Drop at a set date/time. Consider a small in-person moment — a Vancouver gym pop-up or a founder-hosted session — your version of Gymshark's BodyPower booth.
- **Go live across all channels same day.** Founding athletes post in unison. Waitlist gets first access + founding-member pricing.
- **Capture proof.** Real customers in the tee → UGC → repost. Reviews and fit feedback feed the next run.
- **Don't discount.** Protect the premium. If it sells through, that's the story; restock or tease Drop 02.
- **Debrief & roadmap.** What sold, what fit feedback came back, who the real customer is. Tease "more from the source" (the supplements/lifestyle arc) only once apparel has traction.

---

## Metrics that matter (keep it simple)

- Pre-launch: waitlist size (target 300–500), founding athletes confirmed (15–30), saves/shares per post (engagement > follower count).
- Launch: % of first run sold, waitlist→buyer conversion, UGC pieces created.
- Post: repeat interest, fit-feedback themes, press pickups.

## Guardrails (on-brand)

- Voice stays plain, proud, specific — never "beast mode / hustle / luxury."
- Quality over quantity in content too: fewer, better posts.
- Honesty: no fake scarcity, no invented endorsements, real photos for real product.
